Duke Energy Bill Explained — Charges, Usage, Rates, & How to Read Your Statement

Understanding your Duke Energy bill helps you manage payments and reduce monthly costs. Common headings include account summary, current charges, energy usage (kWh) and demand charges where applicable, distribution and delivery fees, taxes and state or municipal surcharges, and any credits or prior balance adjustments. Duke Energy billing may include time-of-use (TOU) rate details, Tier or rate plan information, and meter reading dates. Knowing these components helps explain why a Duke Energy bill went up or down and what actions (energy efficiency, change in thermostat setpoint, time shifting usage) can reduce your Duke Energy monthly bill.

Key billing terms

  • kWh (kilowatt-hour): the unit of energy you are billed for; more kWh = higher energy charge.
  • Delivery/Distribution: cost to move electricity to your home; includes maintenance and infrastructure.
  • Demand/Peak Charges: charges based on highest usage periods for some commercial or time-of-use customers.
  • Taxes & Fees: local, state, and federally required charges.

If you believe a charge is incorrect, call Duke Energy billing help at 877-218-4132 to request account review, usage analysis, or a meter inspection.
